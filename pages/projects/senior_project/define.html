How I Define Myself

My identity is based heavily on my upbringing and my parenthood. My parents are both kind and accepting people, and in many ways they have taught me to be the same. I like to believe that I enter most conflicts giving the benefit of the doubt. I think that trusting in othersâ€™ good faith is key to any confrontation. Many times I find that when I give benefit of the doubt, it gives me a more balanced and objective perspective. Hopefully this will allow me to view my research through an unbiased lens.

Of course there are still unavoidable identities I have to be aware of. I am a white male. Personally, I do not consider either of these as strong elements of my identity; while I am comfortable as a man, I am somewhat indifferent to traditional masculine behaviors and traits, nor do I feel too attached to my heritage as a white European.

However, my identity is not entirely up to me, as my gender and racy carry weight, and I am responsible for that. As a white person, I try to recognize how oppressed groups feel around me, and aim to make them feel safe. During my research, I will inevitably encounter my own unconscious bias. I hope to counter this with conscientious and careful consideration of the repercussions of my words.
