Music Education
in the Time of COVID 19

Research Plan and Methodology

This study utilized qualitative research methodology, and specifically, a narrative inquiry approach (Riessman 2008). There are many different ways to conduct narrative qualitative research. Riessman’s approach is thematic in nature. Thematic narrative approaches to qualitative research “center around the idea that individuals live storied lives, and by recollecting these stories, they make meaning of their lives. Thus, the stories told are analyzed through an inductive process to identify themes of a storyline and represented as such, (Bhattacharya, 2017, p. 94). This approach feels appropriate for my research, as I plan to analyze the specific experiences of teachers and their students as they traverse music education in a virtual landscape. By telling their story, I hope to shed light on the many personal and interpersonal struggles educators go through when adjusting.

Research Plan

Main Question:

How have music educators navigated the COVID 19 pandemic and resulting school closures and partial re-openings, what barriers have they faced, and what strategies have they employed that they deem to be most useful?

Purpose Statement

The purpose of my research is to help identify the struggles and improve the quality of music education in a virtual classroom, with the mindset that music education has a positive impact on students.

Community impact

My research is particularly relevant right now, as many schools are teaching virtually in response to the COVID-19 pandemic. This has been at the price of several music programs, and music education as a whole is suffering. My research should help improve the quality of music education for the community of music educators and students.

How do you identify and define the community that is potentially involved with, and affected by, your inquiry?

I plan to use the PKY website to find a list of active music education classes. I also plan to ask close peers for examples of students who were in music courses last year but not this year. This widens the perspective of the community further.

How does your plan to create visible efforts/events that you facilitate promote collaboration?

I plan to email music teachers to facilitate interviews with music students. I also plan to message several teachers and peers directly to ask for more in depth interviews.

How do you define and describe your plan of action to promote involvement, engagement, and transparency with your community?

My plan involves teachers giving out a survey which includes information about what the answers are being used for. Students will be required to understand this before they are able to answer the questions, which prevents students from answering questions they may not be comfortable answering in some situations.
